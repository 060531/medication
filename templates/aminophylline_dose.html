Dose

Loading dose: 8 mg/kg IV infusion over 30 to 60 minutes, or orally.

Maintenance dose: 1.5 to 3 mg/kg/dose orally, or IV every 8 to 12 hours (start maintenance dose 8 to 12 hours after the loading dose).

In preterm infants, changing from IV aminophylline to oral theophylline requires no dose adjustment.

Administration

Intravenous bolus over 30 to 60 minutes. May give as 25-mg/mL concentration or further dilute to as low as 1 mg/mL for continuous infusion.

Uses

Treatment of neonatal apnea, including post-extubation, post-anesthesia, and prostaglandin E1-induced. Bronchodilator. May improve respiratory function.

Adverse Effects

GI irritation, hyperglycemia, CNS irritability, and sleeplessness. May be associated with renal calcifications when used with furosemide and dexamethasone.

Signs of toxicity: Sinus tachycardia, failure to gain weight, vomiting, jitteriness, hyperreflexia, and seizures.

Treatment of Serious Theophylline Toxicity

Activated charcoal, 1 g/kg as a slurry by gavage tube every 2 to 4 hours. Avoid sorbitol-containing preparations as they may cause osmotic diarrhea.

Monitoring

Monitor heart rate and check blood glucose periodically. Assess for agitation and feeding intolerance.

Consider withholding next dose if heart rate is greater than 180 beats per minute.

When indicated by lack of efficacy or clinical signs of toxicity, serum trough concentration should be obtained. Therapeutic ranges:

  • Apnea of prematurity: 7 to 12 mcg/mL.
  • Bronchospasm: 10 to 20 mcg/mL (older infants with bronchospasm may need higher levels due to increased protein binding).
Pharmacology

Aminophylline stimulates central respiratory drive and peripheral chemoreceptor activity, increasing diaphragmatic contractility. Cerebral blood flow is acutely decreased following IV bolus dose. Renal effects include diuresis and increased urinary calcium excretion. Stimulates gastric acid secretion and may cause gastroesophageal reflux. Cardiac output is increased due to higher sensitivity to catecholamines.

Elimination in preterm infants is primarily as unchanged drug, although significant interconversion to caffeine occurs. The serum half-life of theophylline is prolonged in immature neonates (20 to 30 hours). Theophylline metabolism matures by 55 weeks postmenstrual age.

Special Considerations/Preparation

Aminophylline for IV use (25 mg/mL) is available in 10- and 20-mL vials. Dilute 1 mL (25 mg) with 4 mL NS or D5W to yield a final concentration of 5 mg/mL. Stable for 4 days refrigerated.

Oral aminophylline is no longer available.

Extemporaneous Suspension

Oral aminophylline 3 mg/mL liquid suspension made with aminophylline injection 25 mg/mL and Ora-Sweet solution. Stable for 91 days at 4°C and 25°C.

Solution Compatibility

D5W, D10W, and NSS.

Terminal Injection Compatibility

Compatible with a wide range of antibiotics and other medications such as ampicillin, furosemide, heparin, and more. Incompatible with certain drugs like cefepime, hydralazine, and insulin.
